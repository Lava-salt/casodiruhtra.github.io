What is CaSoDiRuHTra?

CaSoDiRuHTra means Car Social Distance Rule for Heavy Traffic, previously known as CaSoDiRIHeT or CaSoDiRHeT (controversial) which is a rule that you can apply to make automobile traffic go faster. It relies on a set of rules:

Rule #0

CaSoDiRuHTra's rules are only applied when the automobile traffic is slow, boring and reminds people of this.

Rule #1

All cars (applying CaSoDiRuHTra) must social distance each other. They'll social distance the cars on their back/front, but not on their left/right*.
*Cars on their left/right don't affect the traffic flow (except they change lanes).

Rule #2

Changing road lanes is prohibited when applying CaSoDiRuHTra, because doing this is a big reason of heavy traffic.
This includes road crossing slowly or jaywalking, hitchhiking a lot, and driving distractedly*.
*Interacting with devices that make you not look at the road while driving, such as cellular telephones or books.
NOTE: The prohibits are ignored when necessary (e.g. a car accident/construction's on your lane.)

Rule #3

The social distance of Rule #1 can change according to the road type/length/width/density, so the distance will be chosen by CaSoDiRuHTra appliers. This is valid unless these happen:
1. The distance is so short that the CaSoDiRuHTra cars look almost identical to ordinary cars.
2. The distance is so long that cars crossing that time it takes for cars to cross that distance is longer than time they spend on what'd happen without CaSoDiRuHTra.
3. The distance is suspiciously long/short that people question it, you and CaSoDiRuHTra.

Rule #4

While applying Rule #1 of CaSoDiRuHTra, if someone honks you, you must move forward if they don't know CaSoDiRuHTra. You can understand this by the popularity of CaSoDiRuHTra at that time.

Why does it make traffic flow faster?

In a lane where CaSoDiRuHTra isn't applied, and the traffic is heavy, this would be the flow:
1. A car moves forward.
2. The car behind it waits 2-5 seconds (to not crash into that because they're so close)
3. It moves forward, and it returns to 2. step.
So in a 1-lane heavy traffic with 10 cars, the back car needs to wait 20-50 seconds to move.
But in a lane where CaSoDiRuHTra is applied, and the traffic is heavy, this would be the flow:
1. A car moves forward.
2. The car behind it starts moving forward (because they have so much distance so they can't crash.)
3. Both cars social distance each other so CaSoDiRuHTra continues, and it returns to 2. step..
So in a 1-lane heavy traffic with 10 cars, the back car needs to wait ([average human's reaction time] x 10) seconds to move.
